# 2026 Official Tax Rates and Thresholds

This document provides official 2026 tax rates, credits, and deductions for Quebec residents, as used by TaxSyncForDrivers.

## Federal Tax Rates (2026)

### Income Tax Brackets

| Taxable Income | Marginal Tax Rate |
|----------------|-------------------|
| First $58,523 | **14%** *(reduced from 15% in 2025)* |
| $58,523 - $117,045 | 20.5% |
| $117,045 - $181,440 | 26% |
| $181,440 - $258,482 | 29% |
| Over $258,482 | 33% |

**Note:** All brackets indexed approximately 2% for 2026 inflation adjustment.

### Basic Personal Amount (BPA)

- **Maximum BPA:** $16,452
- **Minimum BPA:** $14,829
- **Phaseout range:** $181,440 - $251,440

The BPA is phased out linearly for high-income earners. Tax credit value = BPA × 14%

### Canada Pension Plan (CPP) - 2026

*Not applicable to Quebec residents (see QPP below)*

## Quebec Tax Rates (2026)

### Provincial Income Tax Brackets

| Taxable Income | Marginal Tax Rate |
|----------------|-------------------|
| First $54,345 | 14% |
| $54,345 - $108,680 | 19% |
| $108,680 - $132,245 | 24% |
| Over $132,245 | 25.75% |

**Note:** All brackets indexed approximately 2.05% for 2026 inflation adjustment.

### Basic Personal Amount (Quebec)

- **Amount:** $18,952
- Tax credit value: $18,952 × 14% = **$2,653.28**

### Quebec Pension Plan (QPP) - 2026

#### For Self-Employed Individuals:
- **Base contribution rate:** 13.8% (6.9% employee + 6.9% employer)
- **Maximum pensionable earnings:** $73,200
- **Basic exemption:** $3,500
- **Second additional contribution rate:** 2%
- **Second additional maximum:** $86,700

#### Maximum Contributions:
- Base QPP: ($73,200 - $3,500) × 13.8% = **$9,618.60**
- QPP2 (if income > $73,200): ($86,700 - $73,200) × 2% = **$270.00**
- **Total maximum:** $9,888.60

## Federal Tax Credits (2026)

### Canada Workers Benefit (CWB)

**Eligibility:** Working Canadians with low to moderate income

#### Maximum Benefits:
- **Single (no dependents):** $1,549
- **Family (with dependents):** $2,578

#### Phase-in:
- Below $17,928: 27% of working income

#### Phase-out starts:
- **Single:** $26,050
- **Family:** $39,092

Phase-out rate: 15% above threshold

## Quebec Tax Credits (2026)

### Solidarity Tax Credit (Crédit pour la solidarité)

- **Base amount:** $531 (single) / $1,062 (couple)
- **Phaseout starts:** $57,965
- **Phaseout ends:** $64,125

### Work Premium (Prime au travail)

**Eligibility:** Working Quebecers earning between $7,200 and $57,965

- **Maximum credit (single):** $728
- **Maximum credit (with dependents):** $1,456
- Phase-in rate: 26% of income above $7,200

## Vehicle Deductions (2026)

### Simplified Method (per kilometer)

- **First 5,000 km:** $0.70/km
- **Additional km:** $0.64/km

*Rates for provinces/territories. Add $0.04/km for Yukon, NWT, Nunavut.*

### Detailed Method

Include actual expenses:
- Fuel, insurance, maintenance, registration
- Lease payments or capital cost allowance (CCA)
- Multiply by business-use percentage

**CCA Rate:** Class 10 vehicles - 30% declining balance
**Maximum depreciable cost:** $37,000 (for passenger vehicles)
